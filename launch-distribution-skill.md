# Launch Distribution

**Get your product in front of the right audiences through directories, launch platforms, review sites, and high-value backlinks — and make sure those links land on pages that convert.**

---

## What This Skill Does

This skill helps you plan and execute the distribution layer of a product launch: which directories to submit to, in what order, with what positioning, and what destination pages those links should point at. It also covers Product Hunt strategy, a reviews playbook for G2 and Capterra, and the foundational pages that make directory traffic meaningful.

Directories and launch events are the **foundation layer** of distribution — not the entire strategy. They pass link equity, create discovery surface area, and get you cited by AI engines that pull from high-authority directories. But they don't generate meaningful leads on their own. The destination pages you build — comparison pages, use-case pages, tool pages — do the converting. Build those first.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you want to:

- Plan a product launch
- Submit to directories (startup, SaaS, AI tool, developer, review sites)
- Execute a Product Hunt launch
- Collect early customer reviews on G2, Capterra, or TrustRadius
- Build the comparison, alternative, or use-case pages that earn and convert traffic
- Improve domain authority through backlinks

## Three Rules Before Anything Else

1. **Build your foundation before submitting.** A directory listing pointing at a broken or unconvincing page is worse than no listing. Get the page right first.

2. **Build destination pages before directories.** Links need somewhere useful to land — comparison pages, use-case pages, a free tool. Build them before you start submitting.

3. **Vary your positioning by surface.** Never paste the same description across every directory. Vary the opening and angle for each audience — and AI engines down-weight duplicate content.

## Readiness Checklist

Complete all of these before submitting anywhere:

**Hard blocks — fix before submitting:**
- [ ] Product is publicly accessible, or a working early-access / waitlist page exists
- [ ] A pricing page is live — even "free during beta" counts
- [ ] Privacy policy and terms of service are live
- [ ] Logo assets are ready: PNG, SVG, square 1024×1024, and favicon
- [ ] 5–8 real product screenshots (not mockups) and a 60–90 second demo video
- [ ] Landing pages have clean structure: one H1 per page, sequential headings, FAQ schema markup, basic structured data

**Soft blocks — you can launch, but you'll lose compounding value:**
- [ ] At least 3 comparison/alternative pages and 3 use-case pages are live and indexed
- [ ] A lead magnet or free tool exists (if applicable to your category)
- [ ] ~20 early users who would leave a review if asked

## Directory Tiers

Submit only where your product genuinely fits. Forcing the wrong category gets rejected and wastes the first-submission advantage.

| Tier | When to submit | Examples |
|------|---------------|---------|
| Flagship launch | Launch week | Product Hunt (anchor), BetaList, Hacker News "Show HN" (technical angle required), Fazier, DevHunt |
| Startup / SaaS | Week 1 + ongoing | AlternativeTo, SaaSHub, F6S, SourceForge, Slashdot |
| AI tool directories | Weeks 1–3 (if AI product) | There's An AI For That, Futurepedia, Toolify, Future Tools, and similar AI-tool indexes |
| Agent / developer registries | Weeks 1–3 (if developer tool, plugin, or API) | Relevant registries for your tooling ecosystem; niche, high-intent, low competition |
| Review sites | Launch month | G2, Capterra, TrustRadius — all require reviews to matter (see playbook below) |
| Integration marketplaces | When integrations ship | The marketplace of each tool you integrate with — often your highest-authority backlinks |
| "Best of" listicles | Rolling, ongoing | Cold outreach to high-authority blog roundups in your category |

**Per submission:** Use the audience-appropriate positioning variant, complete the form, upload your assets, submit, and log date / URL / status / notes in a tracker. Once live, verify the backlink is followed (not nofollow).

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1 — Run the Readiness Checklist

Go through every item in the checklist above before submitting anywhere. For any hard block, stop and fix it first. Document which soft blocks exist and note them in the distribution plan.

---

### Step 2 — Build Destination Pages First

Directory links landing on a generic homepage convert poorly. Build these pages before you begin submitting:

**Comparison / alternative pages** (`/alternatives/[competitor]`, `/vs/[competitor]`)
- These convert far better than generic content because buyer intent is high
- Structure: honest feature comparison table, "when to choose them," "when to choose us," pricing comparison, 3–5 use cases, FAQ with schema
- Be truthful — AI engines and buyers cross-check claims; pages that lie get de-ranked and distrust

**Use-case / audience pages** (`/for/[audience]`, `/use-cases/[use-case]`)
- One page per major audience segment, written in their language
- Structure: their trigger, their pain, how you solve it, outcome, relevant proof, objections, CTA

**"Best of" roundups you write** (`/blog/best-[category]-tools`)
- Include competitors honestly; this earns category-query rankings and gets cited
- Aim for genuinely helpful, not promotional

**Integration pages** (`/integrations/[partner]`)
- One per integration when they ship; reliable programmatic SEO

**Template or asset galleries** (if applicable)
- One indexable page per template or asset; strong compounding SEO effect

---

### Step 3 — Execute the Product Hunt Launch

Product Hunt is the highest-leverage startup launch surface, but also the most easily wasted. The algorithm weights comment quality and genuine community engagement over raw upvote counts.

**3-week preparation timeline:**

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Day −21 to −14 | Warm up the account: thoughtfully comment on and upvote 3–5 launches per day; follow active makers; build authentic history |
| Day −14 | Create an "Upcoming" page; drive "notify on launch" subscribers to it via your email list, social, and community |
| Day −7 | Draft all launch assets: gallery images (1270×760px), tagline, ~260-char description, your opening comment, a customer's comment |
| Day −3 | Email your list: "We launch [day]. Reply if you want a personal heads-up." |
| Day −1 | Final check: product works in incognito; video autoplays; CTA leads to signup; listing preview looks correct |

**Launch day execution:**

- Launch at **12:01 a.m. Pacific Time** — this maximizes your 24-hour window on the US-dominant platform
- Launch **Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday** — weekend launches receive 60–70% less traffic
- The first two hours drive algorithmic distribution — aim for 50+ early supporters in that window
- Post your own first comment with the real story: why you built this, what's different, what to try first
- Reply to every comment within ~30 minutes — maker responsiveness is measured
- Share to your email list, social channels, and communities where promotion is welcome
- **Ask for feedback, not upvotes** — "would love your honest take" converts better and doesn't trigger anti-manipulation filters
- Do not cold-DM strangers — this gets flagged and can have your listing hidden

**Post-launch:**
- Publish an honest recap with numbers and lessons on day 2
- Cross-post where promotion is explicitly allowed (Indie Hackers, relevant subreddits)
- Only submit to Hacker News "Show HN" if you have a genuine technical angle — a generic "we launched a SaaS" post will be flagged

---

### Step 4 — Run the Reviews Playbook

Review-site listings without reviews are nearly worthless. On G2, approximately 10 reviews is the threshold for appearing in category reports. Run this protocol during launch month:

**The 10-in-30 Protocol**

1. Identify ~20 users who have completed a meaningful action with your product
2. Email each one a **direct link to your review page** — this removes the most friction
3. A small, genuine thank-you is permitted by most platforms; keep it modest
4. Follow up once after 5 days if no response; don't follow up again after that
5. Target roughly 50% conversion → ~10 reviews from 20 asks

**Notes on review platforms:**
- G2 and Capterra have time-sensitive category report cutoffs — missing one means waiting another quarter for grid inclusion
- A "Users Love Us" badge on G2 is free and requires ~20 reviews at 4.0+
- Paid G2 plans ($2,000+/year) are not worth it in year one — invest elsewhere
- TrustRadius follows similar mechanics; Capterra may auto-populate in some categories

---

### Step 5 — Optimize for AI Search Citation (GEO)

High-authority directory listings feed AI engines, which in turn drive high-intent referral traffic. Reinforce this with on-page signals:

- One H1 per page with clean, sequential heading hierarchy
- Dense, factual content with specific, cited statistics
- FAQ schema markup on every relevant page
- Comparison tables wherever "X vs Y" intent exists
- An explicit "what this product is" paragraph in the first 100 words
- Claimed and current entries on Crunchbase, your LinkedIn company page, and Wikidata
- Genuine mentions in relevant technical communities (Reddit, Hacker News)

Check monthly: ask ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity "what are the best [your category] tools?" and note where you appear and which pages are cited.

## KPIs to Track Weekly

| Metric | Day 0 | ~Day 30 | ~Day 90 |
|--------|-------|---------|---------|
| Domain Rating (Ahrefs/Moz) | baseline | 20+ | 30+ |
| Referring domains | baseline | 30+ | 80+ |
| Indexed pages | — | 50+ | 200+ |
| Directory listings live | 0 | 50 | 70+ |
| G2 / Capterra reviews | 0 | 10 | 25+ |
| AI citations (manual check) | 0 | 3+ | 15+ |
| Signups from directory referrals | 0 | 50+ | 300+ |

If a number stalls, investigate the cause rather than simply submitting to more directories.

## Quality Checks and Guardrails

- [ ] Every submission goes to a page that is live, indexed, and credible — never a 404 or placeholder
- [ ] No description is copy-pasted across directories — each has a varied opening and angle
- [ ] Comparison and alternative pages contain only accurate, verifiable claims
- [ ] Product Hunt launch uses "ask for feedback," never "ask for upvotes"
- [ ] Review requests go only to users who have actually used the product
- [ ] Low-authority or spammy directories are skipped — they dilute your backlink profile
- [ ] No paid directory submission services are used — it's free effort and an afternoon of work
- [ ] Machine-readable files (llms.txt, pricing.md) are kept current if added
