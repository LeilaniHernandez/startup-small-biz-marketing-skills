# Social Media Content

**Create, plan, repurpose, and schedule social content that builds an audience and supports business goals — without a full-time social team.**

---

## What This Skill Does

This skill helps founders, marketers, and small teams produce consistent, effective social media content across LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts. It covers content strategy (pillars, planning, calendars), writing ready-to-post content, scripting short-form video, and repurposing long-form material into a week's worth of posts from a single source.

The approach is built for resource-constrained teams: high quality, low volume, maximum leverage from every piece of content created.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you want to:

- Plan a content strategy or content calendar
- Write posts, threads, carousels, or video scripts
- Repurpose an article, talk, podcast, or webinar into social content
- Grow an audience on one or more platforms
- Build a consistent posting habit with limited time
- Develop a brand or founder voice on social

## Before Creating Content

Establish the following before writing anything. Ask only what isn't already clear:

| Question | Why it matters |
|----------|---------------|
| What's the goal? | Awareness, leads, community, hiring, or brand trust — these produce very different content |
| Who is the audience? | Role, industry, platform, what they care about |
| Which platforms? | Effort should go where the audience actually is |
| What's the tone? | Professional, casual, witty, authoritative — brand or founder voice? |
| What capacity exists? | Hours per week; ability to make video; existing content to repurpose |
| What's been tried? | Avoid repeating what didn't work |

## Platform Quick Reference

| Platform | Best for | Posting cadence | Key formats | Key limits |
|----------|----------|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional audiences, thought leadership | 3–5×/week | Text posts, carousels (8–12 slides), native video | ~1,300 chars before "see more"; links perform better in first comment |
| X / Twitter | Tech, news, communities, real-time conversation | 3–10×/day | Threads, short takes, replies | 280 chars/post |
| Instagram | Visual brands, lifestyle, consumer | 1–2 posts + Stories daily | Reels, photo carousels | Reels: 9:16, 15–30s sweet spot |
| TikTok | Broad awareness, younger audiences | 1–4×/day | Short-form video | 9:16, 15–60s, trending sounds help |
| YouTube Shorts | Searchable how-to content | A few per week | Short-form video | 9:16, searchable titles matter |

For B2B or technical products, LinkedIn and X/Twitter carry the most weight. Short-form video is the highest-reach format on every platform and works well for explaining how something works or why a problem matters.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1 — Define Content Pillars

Content pillars are the 3–5 recurring themes that anchor everything you post. They should sit at the intersection of your expertise and your audience's interests.

A practical early-stage mix:

| Pillar | Target share | What it covers |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Educational | ~30% | Teach the audience something useful — how something works, a concept, a common mistake |
| Industry insight | ~25% | Trends, data, news reactions, predictions in your space |
| Behind-the-scenes | ~20% | How you work, decisions you've made, things you're building |
| POV / opinion | ~20% | Takes, values, contrarian views, founder stories |
| Promotional | ~5% | Product updates, launches, offers |

For each pillar, ask: What unique perspective do we have? What does our audience keep asking? What can we produce consistently? How does this ladder to our business goal?

---

### Step 2 — Write Strong Hooks

The first line determines whether anyone reads, watches, or scrolls past. Every piece of content needs a hook that earns the next sentence.

**Hook categories and formulas:**

**Curiosity hooks**
- "I was wrong about [common belief]."
- "The real reason [outcome] isn't what most people think."
- "[Impressive result] — here's exactly how it happened."

**Story hooks**
- "Last week, something unexpected happened."
- "Three years ago I [past state]. Today, [current state]."
- "I almost made a [costly mistake]. Then I caught it."

**Value hooks**
- "How to [desirable outcome] without [pain point]:"
- "[Number] [things] that [result]:"
- "Stop doing [common mistake]. Do this instead:"

**Contrarian hooks**
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]."
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"
- "Everyone says [thing]. The data says the opposite."

Test different hook types on different pillars and track which earns the most engagement.

---

### Step 3 — Write Platform-Specific Content

**LinkedIn posts**
- Open with a hook on line one (this is what shows before "see more")
- Write in short paragraphs — 1–3 lines maximum
- Use line breaks generously; dense text gets skipped
- End with a question or a clear CTA
- Put links in the first comment, not the post body
- Character count: aim for 150–800 words for high-performing posts

**X / Twitter threads**
- Tweet 1 is the hook — it must work standalone and tease what's coming
- Each subsequent tweet adds one idea, not a summary of multiple ideas
- Tweet 2–3 are the highest-read; front-load the value
- Final tweet: a summary, a CTA, or an open question
- Thread length: 5–12 tweets works well

**Instagram carousels**
- Slide 1 is the hook — treat it like a thumbnail
- Each slide advances one idea; 8–12 slides is the sweet spot
- Use consistent visual design across slides
- Final slide: CTA with a clear next step

### Step 4 — Script Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)

Short-form video is the highest-reach format on every major platform. These frameworks work across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

**The 3-second rule:** You have approximately 3 seconds to stop the scroll. In the very first second, hit three hooks simultaneously: a visual hook, a verbal hook, and a text-overlay hook.

**Video structure templates:**

*Problem → Solution (15–30 seconds)*
```
[0–3s]   HOOK: State the problem directly
[3–10s]  AGITATE: Why this matters / what's at stake
[10–25s] SOLUTION: Your method, tip, or product
[25–30s] CTA: What to do next
```

*List format (30–60 seconds)*
```
[0–3s]   HOOK: "Here are [N] things that [outcome]"
[3–50s]  ITEMS: One per 5–8 seconds, no padding
[50–60s] CTA
```

*Tutorial (30–60 seconds)*
```
[0–3s]   HOOK: Show the end result first
[3–8s]   SETUP: "Here's how to do this..."
[8–50s]  STEPS: Quick, clear, one at a time
[50–60s] RESULT + CTA
```

**Captions/subtitles:** Always add them. Most social video is watched without sound. Use a maximum of 2 lines on screen, 3–5 words per line, bold readable font with a visible outline. Timing should match speech exactly.

**Script delivery format:**
```
HOOK (0–3s):
  Visual: [what's on screen]
  Verbal: [what you say out loud]
  Text overlay: [on-screen text]

[BEAT] [timecode]:
  Visual: [...]
  Verbal: [...]
  Text overlay: [...]

CTA: [final spoken + on-screen text]
```

### Step 5 — Build a Repurposing System

Don't create every post from scratch. Extract from longer content and adapt per platform.

**The system:**

1. Create one "pillar piece" — a blog post, podcast, talk, webinar, or newsletter
2. Extract 5–10 "content atoms" — self-contained moments that work on their own:
   - A quotable claim or memorable line
   - A mini-story with a beginning, middle, and lesson
   - A specific tactical tip
   - A surprising statistic
   - A contrarian take
   - A behind-the-scenes moment
3. Adapt each atom to platform format:
   - LinkedIn text post or carousel
   - X/Twitter thread
   - Instagram Reel or carousel
   - YouTube Short
   - Quote graphic for any platform
4. Write standalone captions — each post must make sense without the others
5. Spread across the week; don't batch-post everything at once

**One blog post can reasonably yield:** 1 LinkedIn post, 1 LinkedIn carousel, 1 X thread, 1–2 short videos, and a quote graphic. That's a week of content from a single source.

Evergreen content can be recycled every 3–6 months.

---

### Step 6 — Plan a Content Calendar

A simple weekly grid prevents the blank-page problem and keeps output consistent.

**Sample weekly grid (adjust to your platforms and capacity):**

| Day | LinkedIn | X / Twitter | Instagram |
|-----|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Monday | Industry insight | Thread | Carousel |
| Tuesday | Behind-the-scenes | Engagement / replies | Story |
| Wednesday | Educational | Tips post | Reel |
| Thursday | POV / opinion | Thread | Educational post |
| Friday | Hot take or question | Engagement / replies | Story |

**Batching:** Set aside 2–3 hours once a week to write ~5 LinkedIn posts, draft 2–3 threads and daily tweets, plan a carousel and a Reel concept, and schedule everything. Keep 1–2 weeks of content queued so you're never posting same-day.

Leave gaps in the queue for real-time, spontaneous posts responding to news or conversations.

---

### Step 7 — Build Engagement Habits

Posting without engaging is broadcasting. A 30-minute daily engagement routine compounds over time:

- Reply to every comment on your own posts (do this within 1–2 hours of posting)
- Thoughtfully comment on 5–10 posts from target accounts — add a real insight, not "great post"
- Reshare content you genuinely value, adding your own take
- Send 2–3 genuine DMs to new connections or people whose content you engaged with

Identify 20–50 accounts in your space and engage with them consistently. Relationships turn into collaborations, co-created content, and audiences who actively share your work.

## Quality Checks and Guardrails

Before finalizing any content:

- [ ] Does the hook earn the next line? Read just the first sentence and ask honestly if you'd keep reading
- [ ] Is every post self-contained? (Each post must make sense without context from other posts)
- [ ] Is the voice consistent with the brand's established tone?
- [ ] Does any claim require a source or qualifier? Add it
- [ ] Have exclamation points been removed or drastically limited?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA — not two or three?
- [ ] Are links kept out of the post body on LinkedIn?
- [ ] For video: is there a text overlay in the first 3 seconds?
- [ ] For video: are captions/subtitles included?
