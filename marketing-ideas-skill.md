# Marketing Ideas

**Find the right marketing tactics for your specific stage, budget, and resources — from a curated library of proven strategies.**

---

## What This Skill Does

This skill helps founders, marketers, and operators find marketing strategies that fit their actual situation — not a generic list of everything that exists. It works from a built-in library of proven tactics organized by category, with selection guides by stage, budget, and goal. The output is a prioritized shortlist with concrete first steps, not a brainstorm dump.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you want to:

- Brainstorm marketing or growth ideas for a product or service
- Find what to try next when current channels aren't working
- Get a starting point for a marketing plan
- Understand what's available by budget, stage, or goal
- Think through which tactics fit your specific business model

**Trigger phrases:** "marketing ideas," "growth ideas," "how do I market this," "marketing strategies," "marketing tactics," "ways to promote my product," "what else can I try," "I'm stuck on growth," "brainstorm marketing," "what marketing should I do," "how do I get more customers"

---

## How to Use This Skill

1. Clarify the situation first: product, audience, stage, budget, team size, and what's already been tried
2. Select **3–5 ideas** from the library that genuinely fit — not the longest list, the best-matched list
3. For each idea, explain: why it fits, the first 2–3 concrete steps, what success looks like, and the resources required
4. Close with a suggested sequence: what to do first, next, and later — so the list becomes a plan

Always bias toward what can be executed *now* with the people and money available.

---

## Before Recommending Ideas

Gather these inputs before diving into the library:

| Input | Question |
|-------|---------|
| Product | What do you sell, and who is it for? |
| Stage | Pre-launch, early (0–100 customers), growth (100–1,000), or scaling (1,000+)? |
| Budget | Roughly what's available per month for marketing? |
| Team | Who can execute — founder only, a small team, or dedicated marketing staff? |
| Already tried | What's been done? What worked? What didn't? |
| Goal | Leads, revenue, awareness, community, or something else? |

## The Idea Library

### Content & SEO

- **Long-tail keyword content** — write for low-competition, high-intent search terms you can realistically rank for in the near term; prioritize queries where someone is actively looking to buy or solve the problem you address
- **Comparison and alternative pages** — create pages targeting "[competitor] alternative" and "X vs Y" searches; these convert far better than generic content because buyer intent is high
- **Category glossary** — define the key terms in your space; builds authority, earns links, and gets cited in AI search answers
- **Programmatic SEO** — generate pages at scale using a template plus data (use cases, integrations, locations, industries)
- **Definitive guide** — write the single best resource on your core topic; earns backlinks and becomes a long-term organic asset
- **Original research or data** — publish findings from your own data or surveys; becomes the source others cite
- **Content repurposing** — turn one long-form piece into multiple posts, threads, short videos, a newsletter section, and a quote graphic
- **Documentation as marketing** — make your docs public, indexable, and excellent; technical buyers research through docs before they buy

### Competitor Strategy

- **Comparison pages** — honest, specific head-to-head pages on your own site; high purchase intent, high conversion
- **Category roundups you write** — include competitors fairly in "best of" content; these rank for category queries and build category authority
- **Migration / switch guides** — make it easy for someone to leave a competitor and come to you

### Free Tools (Engineering as Marketing)

- **ROI or savings calculator** — help prospects quantify the value of your solution before they buy
- **Grader or audit tool** — evaluate something the prospect already has and surface a problem; converts well because it personalizes the issue
- **Generator** — produce a template, policy, name, or config that prospects need; earns search traffic and links
- **Browser extension or CLI tool** — a small free utility that builds awareness, earns word-of-mouth, and qualifies technical buyers

### Paid Advertising

*(Most effective once you have a converting funnel and a baseline of customers)*

- **Search ads** on high-intent keywords — capture demand already in market
- **Retargeting** — reach visitors who showed interest but didn't convert; low-cost, high-intent
- **LinkedIn ads** — precise B2B targeting by job title, company, industry, and seniority
- **Niche newsletter sponsorships** — often cheaper and more targeted than platform ads; choose newsletters your buyers actually read
- **Podcast sponsorships** — builds brand familiarity in a low-competition channel for many B2B niches

### Social Media & Community

- **Founder-led content / build in public** — share the journey, decisions, numbers, and lessons; builds trust and audience faster than brand accounts
- **Education-led posting** — teach the market about the problem you solve; the strongest early organic motion for a new or misunderstood category
- **Short-form video** — the highest-reach format on every platform; works well for explaining problems, showing results, and documenting progress
- **Community participation** — be genuinely, consistently helpful in the forums, subreddits, and Discords where your buyers spend time; 90% contribution, 10% promotion
- **Comment marketing** — add real insight to high-reach posts in your space; builds relationships and earns followers

### Email

- **Founder welcome email** — a personal, direct first message to every new signup; sets tone and opens a two-way channel
- **Onboarding sequence** — guide new users to their first "aha moment"; reduces churn and increases activation
- **Newsletter** — recurring value delivered consistently; keeps you top-of-mind until the prospect is ready to buy
- **Re-engagement campaign** — bring dormant leads or users back with something genuinely new or useful

### Partnerships

- **Integration marketing** — co-market with tools your product integrates with; their marketplace is often a high-authority backlink and a qualified audience
- **Affiliate or referral programs** — let satisfied customers and partners earn by referring new ones
- **Newsletter audience swaps** — trade a mention with complementary (non-competing) newsletters; zero-cost reach to a targeted audience
- **Co-created content** — joint guides, webinars, or research with a partner; both audiences, shared effort

### Events

- **Webinars and live demos** — teach something, then show the product; converts well because both intent and attention are high
- **Conference speaking** — authoritative positioning and pipeline in professional, regulated, or technical markets
- **Virtual summits or roundtables** — convene your audience around the problem; positions you as a leader in the space
- **Office hours or AMAs** — low-cost, high-trust; builds relationships at scale

### PR & Media

- **Earned media** — pitch journalists a genuine news angle or exclusive data; one good placement can drive awareness for months
- **Guest posts and bylines** — publish thought leadership in industry publications your buyers read
- **Data-driven PR** — your original research or data becomes the hook for press coverage

### Launches and Moments

- **Product Hunt / BetaList** — the flagship launch event for consumer and SaaS products; requires preparation and community warmup to work well
- **Waitlist with referral** — turn pre-launch interest into organic reach by rewarding referrals
- **Early-access or founding-member pricing** — reward the first cohort; creates urgency and initial social proof
- **Public changelog** — ship in the open; every update is a content moment and a reason to contact inactive users

### Product-Led Growth

- **Free tier or freemium** — let the product demonstrate its own value; reduces friction to first use
- **Sharing and viral loops** — design outputs or results that naturally expose new users to the product
- **"Powered by" branding** — light attribution on free outputs; turns users into distribution
- **In-product upgrade nudges** — contextual prompts at the moment of value, not on a timer

### Content Formats

- **Podcast (guest tour or own show)** — builds relationships and deep authority in a space over time
- **Email course** — deliver value over a series of emails; generates leads and teaches the market
- **Annual report or "state of [category]"** — a recurring research asset that earns coverage every year
- **Templates and swipe files** — instantly useful, highly shareable, natural lead magnets

### Platforms and Directories

- **App and integration marketplaces** — list in the marketplaces of the tools you connect with
- **AI tool directories** — for AI-native products, these are high-intent and far less saturated than general SaaS directories
- **Review platforms** — G2, Capterra, TrustRadius; essential for B2B credibility, but only valuable with real reviews

### Developer-Specific

- **Developer relations and advocacy** — show up helpfully where developers are: conferences, Discord, forums, YouTube, technical meetups
- **Open-source contribution or tool** — earns goodwill, inbound links, and developer adoption
- **Technical deep-dives** — detailed architecture or engineering posts earn respect, links, and trust from technical buyers

### Audience and Word-of-Mouth

- **Referral program** — make it rewarding and easy for happy customers to refer others
- **Customer-language marketing** — identify the exact words your buyers use and use them verbatim in copy, ads, and content
- **Case studies and customer stories** — proof in the buyer's own industry and context; the most persuasive content for high-consideration purchases
- **Design-partner or beta program** — recruit early customers who co-develop the product and become its first advocates

## Choosing by Constraint

### By stage

| Stage | Best bets |
|-------|----------|
| Pre-launch | Waitlist + referral, early-access pricing, directory preparation, founder social content, a free tool that demonstrates the problem |
| Early (0–100 customers) | Long-tail content, comparison pages, education-led social, community participation, founder-led outreach |
| Growth (100–1,000 customers) | Paid acquisition, partnerships and integrations, webinars and events, referral programs |
| Scaling (1,000+) | Brand campaigns, PR and media, original research, international expansion |

### By budget

| Budget | Best bets |
|--------|----------|
| Free | Long-tail content, community participation, social media, founder-led posting, comparison pages, outreach |
| Low ($500–$2,000/month) | Targeted search ads, niche newsletter sponsorships, no-code free tool |
| Medium ($2,000–$10,000/month) | Events, partnerships, PR, paid social |
| High ($10,000+/month) | Brand campaigns, conferences, paid at scale, PR firm |

### By timeline

| Timeline | Best bets |
|----------|----------|
| Quick wins (this week) | Ads, directory submissions, email campaigns, social posts |
| Medium-term (1–3 months) | Content and SEO, community building, a free tool |
| Long game (3+ months) | Brand, thought leadership, original research, platform effects |

### By goal

| Goal | Best bets |
|------|----------|
| Leads fast | Search ads, a free grader or calculator with email capture, comparison pages |
| Build authority | Original research, conference speaking, a podcast tour, definitive guides |
| Low-budget growth | Long-tail content, community participation, comment marketing, founder social |
| Product-led growth | Free tier, sharing loops, in-product upgrade moments |
| Enterprise or regulated buyers | Case studies, compliance and security content, review platform presence, design-partner program |
| Developer audience | Open-source tool, DevRel, technical deep-dives, community participation |

## Quality Checks and Guardrails

Before finalizing recommendations:

- [ ] Are the suggested ideas genuinely executable with the resources described — or are they aspirational?
- [ ] Has what's already been tried been accounted for? (Don't recommend things that have already been ruled out)
- [ ] Is the list 3–5 ideas, not 15? (Fewer, better-chosen ideas outperform long lists)
- [ ] Does each idea have a concrete first step — not just a description?
- [ ] Is there a suggested sequence, so the list becomes an action plan?
- [ ] Are any ideas in tension with each other? (Flag this rather than recommend both)
- [ ] For budget-constrained situations, are free or low-cost ideas prioritized?

## Expected Output Format

For each recommended idea:

```
### [Idea Name]
Why it fits: [specific connection to their stage, audience, or constraints]
How to start: [first 2–3 concrete, actionable steps]
What success looks like: [a specific, measurable outcome]
Resources needed: [time estimate / budget / skills required]
```

Close with a sequenced plan:

```
## Suggested Sequence

**Start now:**
- [Idea 1] — [one-line reason]
- [Idea 2] — [one-line reason]

**Add in 30–60 days:**
- [Idea 3] — [one-line reason]

**Revisit at [milestone]:**
- [Idea 4] — [what condition makes this the right time]
```
